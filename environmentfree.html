The Science of Climate Change

Climate change refers to long-term alterations in temperature, weather patterns, and ecosystems due to human activities, primarily the burning of fossil fuels. This releases greenhouse gases like carbon dioxide, which trap heat in the atmosphere, leading to global warming, rising sea levels, and disruptions in ecosystems and biodiversity.

Biodiversity and Conservation

Biodiversity refers to the variety of life on Earth, including species, ecosystems, and genetic diversity. Conservation is the protection and management of this biodiversity to prevent extinction, habitat loss, and degradation. It is essential for maintaining ecosystem stability, supporting food security, and sustaining natural resources for future generations.

Introduction to Environmental Law and Policy

Environmental law and policy regulate human activities to protect the environment. It includes laws on air and water quality, waste management, biodiversity conservation, and climate change. These regulations aim to balance economic development with environmental sustainability, ensuring the protection of natural resources for current and future generations.

Global Environmental Management

Global environmental management involves strategies and practices to address environmental challenges across borders. It includes international cooperation, policies, and agreements to protect ecosystems, reduce pollution, and promote sustainable development. Key areas include climate change, biodiversity conservation, and resource management, requiring collective action to ensure a sustainable planet for future generations.
